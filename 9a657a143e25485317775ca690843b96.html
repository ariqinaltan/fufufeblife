HOW OFTEN SHOULD YOU WASH SILK PILLOWCASES?

When you think about it, we spend almost one third of our life in contact with our pillowcase, so washing it regularly is important. It’s recommended to wash your pillowcase every two or three days – at the very least weekly – to ensure the area stays clean from makeup and sweat build-up, which could then have an impact on your skin’s health.

ARE SILK PILLOWCASES WORTH IT?

Due to the luxe material they are made from, silk pillowcases are often more costly than your average bed linen. However, the material comes with benefits other than just simply feeling fantastic. When we sleep, we toss and turn creating lots of friction and this friction can impact us in two ways – it tosses our hair around, and pulls our skin. Due to the smooth surface, silk pillowcases create less friction, in turn theoretically improving hair and skin. They are also hypo-allergenic, breathable and chemical-free, making them perfect for sensitive skin and those who suffer from allergies. The silk also regulates heat meaning it is cool on skin, helping you sleep better.

While there isn’t much research in silk pillowcases themselves, experts believe there is no harm trying to add them to your routine as a supplement along with other hair and skin treatments.

CAN YOU SLEEP WITH WET HAIR ON A SILK PILLOWCASE?

Sleeping with wet hair on your silk pillowcase can actually be very beneficial. Unlike cotton that often robs your hair of moisture, silk won’t absorb your lock’s moisture allowing it to stay more hydrated while the slippery surface can help keep strands from tangling reducing frizz.

HOW SHOULD YOU WASH YOUR SILK PILLOWCASE?

Always read the label for instructions on your particular product but generally speaking, silk pillowcases are machine washable on a cool/delicate cycle. It’s recommended to put it in a delicate wash bag and to use a pH neutral liquid detergent and/or fabric softener. Do not tumble dry and only iron on low heat.
